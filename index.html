1TRV - three different pricing structures
Unlike PVPC in Spain (one regulated tariff with hourly pricing), France's regulated TRV comes in three structural options a household chooses between when signing up. Same regulator, same underlying tariff, different time-of-use logic.
Base
One flat price per kWh, all day, every day. Simplest option, no behavioral optimization possible or needed.
✅ Single kWh rate
✅ Fixed annual subscription (by kVA)
HPHC
Heures Pleines / Heures Creuses - two rates, peak and off-peak, at fixed times of day set by the supplier/grid operator.
✅ HP rate (peak hours)
✅ HC rate (off-peak, cheaper)
Tempo
The most complex structure: each calendar day is colored Bleu, Blanc, or Rouge in advance, each with its own HP/HC rates. Rouge days are rare but expensive.
✅ 6 rate combinations (3 colors x HP/HC)
✅ Day color announced the evening before

3What's inside the bill
Same logic as PVPC in Spain: the regulated price bundles energy with network costs. In France, the network component has its own name and its own update cycle - TURPE.
TURPE 7 - the network tariff
TURPE (Tarif d'Utilisation des Réseaux Publics d'Électricité) is the fee for using the electricity grid - paid by every supplier and passed on to every household, regardless of which supplier or tariff structure they're on. Updated periodically (currently TURPE 7) and a recurring "what changes" report trigger on its own.
1PRVG - the regulated gas reference price
PRVG (Prix Repère du Gaz) is the gas equivalent of TRV - published monthly by the CRE as a reference, not a tariff households must use (the regulated gas tariff itself was abolished in France; PRVG is now a benchmark suppliers' offers are compared against).
Two consumption profiles
B0 (Cuisson + Eau) covers cooking and hot water only - lower annual consumption. B1 (Chauffage) adds gas heating on top - much higher annual consumption, and therefore a much larger bill impact when the price per kWh moves.

4ATRD 7 - the gas network tariff
Same concept as TURPE, applied to gas distribution. ATRD (Accès des Tiers au Réseau de Distribution) is the fee for using the gas distribution network - currently in its 7th version, updated periodically like TURPE.
For real, current figures: TRV, PRVG, TURPE and ATRD rates update on their own schedules and shift with policy. This page teaches the structure - for actual calculations, use the dedicated Excel models (TRV tracker, PRVG tracker, French bill model, and the others from the Models project).
VAT on energy is the headline tax lever in France, alongside the periodic network tariff updates (TURPE, ATRD) that aren't taxes exactly but function the same way in a report - a regulatory line item that moves the bill regardless of the wholesale price.

2Why network tariffs matter as much as taxes
TURPE and ATRD aren't taxes, but they behave like one in a report: they're set by regulatory decision, updated on a schedule independent of wholesale prices, and they move every household's bill the same way taxes do. A TURPE 7 update is exactly as newsjacking-worthy as a VAT change.

Reading the curve
2023-2024 shows the sharpest move in the dataset: Base 6kVA jumps +24.7% in 2023 then another +16.0% in 2024, before correcting -21.1% in 2025 as the crisis eased and relief measures landed. Tempo behaves differently - it actually dropped in 2022 (-15.1%) while Base and HPHC were climbing, because Tempo's pricing model responds to a different mix of signals.
Two concepts explain most reader confusion about French energy bills: why network tariffs (TURPE/ATRD) don't move with wholesale prices, and why a small per-kWh difference in PRVG profiles produces a huge euro gap once you multiply by real consumption.

1Why consumption profile matters more than price per kWh
This is the single most useful concept for writing PRVG reports. Chauffage (B1) has a lower price per kWh than Cuisson (B0) - but consumes roughly 14x more energy. The bill impact runs entirely in the opposite direction of the per-kWh price.
Cuisson + Eau (B0)
Lower annual consumption (~4,000 kWh), higher price per kWh. Small absolute bill, but the percentage swing from a price change is very visible.
Chauffage (B1)
Higher annual consumption (~11,200 kWh), lower price per kWh. Same percentage price change produces a far larger euro impact on the bill.
Report angle: "PRVG rises just 0.7% for Chauffage households - but that's worth 3x more in euros than the same percentage for a Cuisson-only home." The consumption multiplier is the story, not just the price.
2Why TURPE/ATRD changes are a story on their own
When TURPE or ATRD updates, the wholesale energy price hasn't necessarily moved at all - it's a pure regulatory shift, fully separate from PRVG or wholesale electricity prices. This is what makes "TURPE 7: what it means for your bill" a clean, self-contained report that doesn't need any market drama to justify itself.
France has more active household energy aid schemes than Spain's single Bono Social - and most are under-used, which is itself a recurring report angle.
⚠️
Sample eligibility and amounts. Aid scheme rules, amounts, and deadlines change yearly with the budget - confirm current figures before publishing.
1The main schemes
Chèque Énergie
The direct equivalent of Spain's Bono Social - an annual payment for low-income households, sent automatically (no application needed if eligible based on tax declaration) and usable for any energy bill or certain renovation works. Distributed in batches, typically arriving around November.
MaPrimeRénov
A renovation subsidy for insulation, heating system upgrades, and other energy efficiency works. Amount depends on income bracket and the specific work done. Rules get updated periodically - "what changed this month" is a recurring, reliable report format.
CEE (Certificats d'Économies d'Énergie)
A scheme requiring energy suppliers to fund efficiency works for customers - in practice, often stacked on top of MaPrimeRénov. Consistently flagged as under-utilized by households who don't realize they qualify.
2Tax credits and declarations
Energy renovation costs (MaPrimeRénov-adjacent works, CEE-funded works) can interact with the annual tax declaration period - a reliable seasonal trigger for a reminder-style report each spring.
Knowing who publishes what, and when the recurring calendar moments are, is what lets you go from "I have an idea" to "I have a number" in minutes instead of hours.
